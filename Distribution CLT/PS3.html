# Make a function to get the mean and the variance for later uses
def get_mean_var(x):
    mean_var = []
    mean_var.append(np.mean(x))
    return mean_var

# Make a function to get the R number of trails of size s of random drawing of 1 and -1
def get_random(s):
    Rpairs = []
    mean_Rpairs = []
    for i in range(0, R):
        current_trial = np.random.randint(0, 2, size = s) * 2 - 1
        Rpairs.append(current_trial)
        Rpairs_mean_var = get_mean_var(current_trial)
        mean_Rpairs.append(Rpairs_mean_var[0])
    return(mean_Rpairs)

# Create Rpairs of random realization of sample size 2 and calcultae mean and variance

12. (8pt) Explain why do the histograms resemble normal distribution as Sgrows. 
In particular, explain what happens when we move from single values S=1 to pairs S=2. Why did two equal peaks turn into a -shaped histogram?

When S=1, our random variable X has a 0.5 probability of having a mean of -1 (tails) and a 0.5 probability of having a mean of 1 (heads). When we increase the size from 1 to 2, there are more combinations. We can have head-head, head-tail, tail-head, and tail-tail. In term of numbers, we will have (1, 1), (1, -1), (-1, 1), (-1, -1) and the means of those sets are 1, 0, 0, and -1. This explains why that extra bar is in the middle and makes the equal peaks into a shaped histogram. As we increase the sizes, the combinations of heads and tails also increase and change the possible means, making the distribution more normal.
